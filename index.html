The Thesis

A Durable Industry, Hiding in Plain Sight

Roughly 71 million U.S. households own a dog — and they spend on care the way they spend on essentials, not luxuries. Boarding and daycare behave like a recurring household need, not a splurge. That is durable demand.

Yet this industry has never had an institutional capital partner built for it — no underwriting designed around how these businesses work, no capital-markets channel. The demand is institutional-grade; the financial infrastructure didn't exist. Canine Capital is building it.

The vehicle is private credit — capital allocated directly to businesses, outside public exchanges. These markets have grown rapidly, driven by the pursuit of durable income and greater flexibility than public markets allow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Fixed-income bonds issued by Canine Capital, an AI-native non-bank financial institution focused on the businesses that care for America's dogs. Proceeds fund asset-backed commercial credit facilities, and bondholders receive a fixed contractual rate for the bond term via their chosen payment election.
Eligibility depends on the series: the 2026 vintage (Series A, B, C, and the Promissory Note) requires verified accredited investors under Reg D 506(c); the 2027 Retail series are planned under Reg A+ Tier II with broader access including retirement accounts; the 2027 INTL series are limited to non-U.S. investors under Reg S; and the Immigration & Jobs Bond™ serves EB-5 Regional Center participants.
Two layers. Layer one: the operating cash flow of borrowing businesses, providing income-based repayment coverage. Layer two: real estate and other hard collateral assets, providing asset-based recovery in default scenarios.
Cash-Pay pays contractual monthly fixed-income payments to your account during the term. Growth compounds those payments monthly and pays the accumulated value at maturity. Most series offer both; the perpetual SD-IRA series (2026 Series C and 2027 Retail C) are Growth-only, and the Promissory Note and EB-5 bond are Cash-Pay.
Two perpetual Growth-election series are built for retirement accounts: 2026 Series C (accredited, from $50,000) and 2027 Retail Series C (from $10,000). Both may be held in a Self-Directed IRA, Roth IRA, Traditional IRA, SEP IRA, Solo 401(k), HSA, or via an eligible rollover. Consult your advisor regarding suitability.
