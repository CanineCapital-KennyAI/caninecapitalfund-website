02 · A Foundation of American Small Business

For 70 years, the SBA has been how Main Street buys, builds, and owns.

Since 1953, the U.S. Small Business Administration has stood behind lenders so they can say yes to small businesses that banks alone would pass on. Two programs do most of that work — and both fit a boarding or kennel business.

The Flagship

SBA 7(a)

General-purpose financing, partially guaranteed by the SBA

  • The SBA's most-used program — working capital, equipment, real estate, and business acquisition under one roof.
  • Longer terms and a lower equity injection than most conventional loans, because the SBA guarantee de-risks the lender.
  • Ideal for buying an existing kennel, expanding capacity, or refinancing growth.
The Fixed-Asset Program

SBA 504

Long-term, fixed-rate financing for major fixed assets

  • Built for owner-occupied real estate and heavy equipment — the kennel building, the land, the runs.
  • Delivered with a Certified Development Company alongside a bank, with a long fixed rate that protects your payment for decades.
  • Lets an operator own the real estate the business runs on — not rent it.
